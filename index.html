Saudi Oil History

This is the Story of Oil in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

On May 29, 1933, the company "Standard Oil" drilled 10 wells, after signing the agreement with the Government of the Arab Kingdom

in 1935

The company chose the dome of Dammam to be the headquarters of the drilling

The company drilled 10 wells, but the results were disappointing, and the company chose to leave after five years of frustration in Saudi Arabia



March 4, 1938

Continue drilling well No.7

At fist there was no results , but one of the American geologists did not lose hope. As the equipment continued to drill at the famous Well No. 7 of unprecedented levels, on the morning of March 4, 1938 oil flowed from the well in commercial quantities



In 1944

A move towards enhaning the Kingdom's role in oil production

In 1944,a move aimed at bolstering the Kingdom's role in oil production, the company's name was changed from Kasuk to the Arab American Oil Company, which became known as Aramco



in 1950

The company completed the Tabline

To become the longest oil pipeline in the world, with a length of slightly more than 1200 km, where oil was transferred from the Arabian Gulf to the Mediterranean Sea



80 Years History

From the dicovery of well No.7

This well made the oil giant Aramco one of the largest companies in the world, through production and pumping of more than 10 million barrels per day,the management of more than 260 billion barrels of crude oil and 284 trillion standard cubic feet



Today

Achivements

The company was able to discover 116 oil fields, in conjunction with pumping oil at commercial quantity from well No.7.The company dicoverd Ghawar which is one of the largest oilfields in the world, along with the Safaniya field and other big oil fields in Saudi Arabia

When did the Market reached it's peak?

In April 2015, the Saudi oil minister Ali Al-Naimi said that Saudi Arabia produced 12,630,000 million barrels per day in March that year, which was the highest figure based on records since the early 1980s. The previous peak was in August 2013 at 12,730,000 million barrels per day.
